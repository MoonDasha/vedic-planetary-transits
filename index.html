Vedic astrology transit calculator for daily sidereal planetary movement

If you have been searching for a Vedic astrology transit calculator that lets you watch the planets move instead of studying a static list, you are in the right place. This player follows daily sidereal planetary positions from 1950 to 2050 and connects every longitude with its zodiac sign, nakshatra and pada.

Use the animation to notice sign changes, retrograde periods, conjunctions and shifts in planetary speed. The stars can illuminate timing and patterns, but they do not remove personal choice. A transit is most useful when it becomes a starting point for thoughtful observation.

How to use the daily planetary transit player

Select a year, press play, or choose an exact date from the calendar. Each animation step represents one day. The table below the charts gives the same data in a precise format, including degrees within the sign, absolute longitude, nakshatra, pada, daily speed and motion state.

The circular wheel places 0° Aries at the top and increases longitude clockwise. It is the clearest view for studying exact angular distance. Turn on motion trails when you want to follow a selected planet across several recent days.

Read transits in a North Indian Vedic astrology chart

The North Indian chart keeps the twelve houses fixed. Choose an ascendant to place a sign in the first house, then the remaining signs follow through the chart. Use House Jump for a compact house-based layout, or Continuous motion to place each planet along a 0° to 30° path through its current sign and house.

This view is useful when you want to connect a transit with house themes. Remember that the ascendant selector is manual. For a personal interpretation, use the ascendant from an accurately calculated birth chart.

Zodiac signs, nakshatras and padas explained

The sidereal zodiac contains twelve signs of 30° each. Vedic astrology also divides the full circle into twenty-seven nakshatras, or lunar mansions, measuring 13°20′ each. Every nakshatra contains four padas of 3°20′.

The sign describes the broader field of expression. The nakshatra gives a finer layer of symbolism and motivation, while the pada narrows the position further. Watching all three together reveals details that a sign-only transit list can miss.

Direct, retrograde, stationary and combust planets

A direct planet advances through zodiac longitude. A retrograde planet appears to move backward from Earth, while a stationary planet is changing direction so slowly that its daily motion is almost zero. The player derives these states from consecutive daily positions and animates the correct direction across 0° Aries.

A small C marks planets within the configured combustion distance from the Sun. The Moon and lunar nodes are not treated as combust here. No single condition should be interpreted in isolation. In Jyotish, a transit gains meaning through the natal chart, planetary dignity, aspects, house rulership and the active dasha period.

Common questions

Vedic transit calculator FAQ

Does this transit calculator use the sidereal zodiac?

Yes. The player displays the supplied sidereal longitudes through all twelve zodiac signs and twenty-seven nakshatras, beginning with 0° Aries.

What is the difference between the circular wheel and North Indian chart?

The circular wheel emphasizes exact longitude, zodiac and nakshatra movement. The North Indian chart groups planets into fixed houses according to the ascendant you select.

Can I view Vedic planetary transits for a specific date?

Yes. Choose any available year from 1950 to 2050, select a date from the calendar, or drag the daily timeline to the required record.

Does this tool replace a personal birth chart reading?

No. Transit positions become personal when they are compared with your natal chart, house lords, dashas and the broader chart context. Use this tool as a clear timing reference rather than an absolute prediction.

Positions are read directly from the local ephemeris files. Motion state and daily speed are derived from consecutive dates.
